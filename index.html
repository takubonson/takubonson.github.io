About me

T akubon Son is a graduate student at the Graduate School of Interdisciplinary Information Studies at the University of Tokyo. He is currently conducting research on text-to-3D generation. His main interest is to develop methods that can produce refined 3D models from plain text inputs. Takubon Son is particularly interested in this field because he believes that it can enable novel and creative ways of content creation and content customization. He also aims to explore the possibility of combining text-to-3D models with realistic human avatar creation, which is another challenging and exciting research area. Specifically, he explores how to use text inputs to customize 3D avatar outfits, allowing users to express their preferences and styles in a natural and intuitive way. He believes that this can facilitate more personalized 3D avatar creation and enhance the user experience in various applications. His long-term vision is to use generative models to assist artists with content creation and content customization in the fields of virtual reality, 3D animation, and 3D games. He hopes that his research can contribute to the advancement of these fields and to the society at large.
